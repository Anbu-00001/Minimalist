## 1. General hackathon-winning patterns (judges, presentation, README, storytelling)

### Devpost — judge perspectives (fetched directly)
- URL: https://info.devpost.com/blog/hackathon-judging-tips ("How to win a hackathon: Advice from 5 seasoned judges")
  - Quote (Richard Moot, Square): "The demo video gives the most amount of scope" and judges look for submissions that "balance across all the judging criteria."
  - Quote (Karen Bajza-Terlouw, Databricks): "The first thing I do is go over the requirements" and "creativity or enthusiasm" signals quality work. "Presentation and storytelling matters."
  - Quote (Kelvin Boateng, Google): "The first thing we look at is how visually appealing the project is." Flutter judges evaluate "the end-user experience" over code quality initially.
  - Quote (Maria Yarotska, NEAR Foundation): Focuses on "the pitch and description" and entrepreneurial mindset over pure technical excellence. (Paraphrase: judges assess whether projects meet basic criteria, then conduct technical reviews.)
  - Quote (Warren Marusiak, Atlassian): Initial screening checks "if the application installs, whether the basic premise is solid, and whether it works at all." The finished product should be "something that I would want to use."
- URL: https://info.devpost.com/blog/understanding-hackathon-submission-and-judging-criteria
  - Quote: "Judges assess how well you've utilized the required tools and technologies. They'll also consider the complexity and technical sophistication of your project." (Technological Implementation criterion)
  - Quote: "A great project isn't just technically impressive, it's also user-friendly. Judges will look for an intuitive design and a smooth user experience." (User Experience criterion)
  - Quote: "Your demo video is your chance to shine. It should clearly and concisely explain your project, its features, and its benefits." (Demonstration criterion)
  - Quote: "Does your project solve a genuine problem? Does it have the potential to make a positive impact?" (Problem-Solving Impact)
  - Quote: "Originality and innovation are highly valued in hackathons. Don't be afraid to think outside the box."
  - Paraphrase: the article stresses that meeting eligibility requirements and following submission guidelines is equally critical — many submissions get disqualified simply for not meeting baseline criteria.
- URL: https://info.devpost.com/blog/6-tips-for-making-a-hackathon-demo-video
  - Paraphrase (via search snippet): most demo videos are under three minutes long; write a script beforehand; a good rule of thumb is 60% of the time explaining, 40% actually demoing. Don't wait until the last minute to upload the video and check privacy settings — if it's private, judges can't access it.
